# ACCE Student Portal — How It Would Work

Hi Priyanka — here's the plan for the new student portal in plain terms. Have a read and let us know what you think, especially about the **booking / time slots** part, which is the bit that affects your day-to-day the most.

## The big idea

Right now everything happens over WhatsApp — students message you, you sort out a time, and payment is separate. The portal turns this into one tidy place where students log in, **book a session, and pay upfront** before the session is confirmed. That means no more no-shows where someone books your time and doesn't turn up or pay.

Later on it grows into a proper membership site (practice questions, video walkthroughs, a student Q&A forum), but **step one is bookings and payment** — that's what we build first.

## Group classes — building this first

Your Test 3 revision classes are exactly what the portal is best at, so we're making **group classes the very first thing we build**. Here's why it's a no-brainer: it's the same amount of your time, but several students pay for the same slot — the maths is way stronger than 1-on-1, and it's actually simpler to run online.

How it works:

- You create each class in your admin area — **subject, date & time, how many seats (e.g. 4–6), and the price per seat**. (Just like the 6 classes on your Test 3 flyer.)
- Students see a list of upcoming classes, each showing **how many spaces are left** ("2 seats left") — same "spaces are limited" feeling as your poster, but automatic.
- They **pay to grab a seat**. Once it's full, it closes itself — no more manual tracking of who's paid and who's coming.
- Everyone in the class gets **one shared Google Meet link** (or the venue address for in-person).
- You get a clean list of who's enrolled per class, so you can prep your lesson plan and notes for the room.

## How students book a 1-on-1 session

1. They log in and choose their **level** (e.g. Undergrad or CTA/PGDA), the **subject** (Accounting, Management Accounting & Finance, Auditing, or Tax), and **how long** they want the session.
2. The price shows automatically (based on your rates — see below).
3. They pick a **time from your available slots**.
4. They can **write out what they're struggling with** and even **upload a photo or document** of the work they want to go through — so you can prep before the session.
5. They **pay** (or use credit they've already bought), and the session is confirmed.

**Payment is taken upfront, every time.** For online sessions, a **Google Meet link is created automatically** and sent to the student (it also lands on your own calendar). For in-person, it shows the location instead.

## How YOU control your availability — the important bit

**You are in full control of when you're available. Nothing gets booked unless you've opened the time.**

- In your admin area, you set your **available windows** — e.g. _"Tuesday 2pm–6pm, online"_ or _"Thursday 10am–1pm, in-person at [location]."_
- You can set a **repeating weekly pattern** (set it once, e.g. every Tue & Thu afternoon) and then just **block out** the odd day when you're not around.
- Students can **only** book inside the windows you've opened.
- When a student books part of a window, that time **automatically closes** — the next student only sees what's still free. You don't have to close anything manually.

So a 2pm–6pm window naturally fills up as students book, and once it's full it disappears. If you're away, you simply don't open windows (or you block the time).

**One thing we'd like your opinion on:** to avoid ending up with useless little gaps (like a spare 30 minutes between two sessions that's too short to do anything with), we can make the system nudge students to book on the hour/half-hour so your day stays neat. Do you like that idea? And what's the **shortest session** you'd ever want to teach (30 min? 1 hour?)? That helps us set the rules sensibly.

## Your prices

Prices depend on the **level** and the **length** of the session (the subject doesn't change the price). We'll give you a simple table in your admin area where you can set and change these yourself anytime — no developer needed. **Could you send us your current rates** (e.g. what you charge for a 1-hour undergrad session vs a 1-hour CTA session, etc.) so we can set it up?

## Packages & credit

Students can buy a **package upfront** (e.g. pay for several sessions at once, with a small bonus for doing so) and that sits as a **credit balance**. When they book, it comes off their balance. Good for regulars and better for cash flow.

## If a student cancels

To protect your time, we'd suggest this refund policy (tell us if you'd change it):

- Cancel **48+ hours** before → **full refund** to their balance.
- Cancel **24–48 hours** before → **70% back** (you keep 30% for the held time).
- Cancel **less than 24 hours** before, or no-show → **no refund**.

The system enforces this automatically.
